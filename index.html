Privacy Policy for Polytown Extension

Last updated: May 12, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to Polytown Extension (“we,” “us,” or “our”), created and maintained by Jad Mintun. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle any information (if any) when you use our browser extension.

2. Extension Details

  • Name: Polytown
  • Version: Beginning with 0.0.38
  • Manifest Version: 3
  • Chrome URL Override: newtab → newpage.html
  • Default Icons:
    • 48×48: img/icon48.png
    • 128×128: img/icon128.png
  • Content Security Policy: 
    extension_pages:
  "script-src 'self' 'wasm-unsafe-eval'; object-src 'self'"
sandbox:
  "sandbox allow-scripts allow-forms allow-popups allow-modals; script-src 'self' 'unsafe-inline' 'unsafe-eval'; worker-src 'self'"

3. Information We Do Not Collect

Polytown Extension does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data. In particular:

  • We do not collect names, email addresses, or any user identifiers.
  • We do not track browsing history or page contents.
  • We do not use analytics or third‑party cookies.

4. Permissions and Data Use

Our extension requests only the minimal permissions required for its core functionality, as defined in the manifest. No data leaves your browser context beyond what you explicitly trigger (e.g., opening a new tab).

5. Third‑Party Services

We do not integrate with any third-party services or APIs that collect user data.

6. Security

We follow best practices for browser extension security:

  • All scripts are loaded with strict Content Security Policy rules.
  • No remote code execution is performed.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make substantive changes, we will update the “Last updated” date above. Your continued use of the extension after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
